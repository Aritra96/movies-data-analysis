Introduction¶

This report explores the TMDb (Movies Dataset) data which has a list of ~11000 movies from IMDB. In addition to regular movies, it also features TV series and short films. The dataset itself has a number of variables including, but not limited to, budget, revenue, popularity, et al. Based on these factors, the following questions will be investigated:

1. What is the relationship between revenue and popularity, revenue and vote count, revenue and vote average, and revenue and runtime?

2. Does the release date (month) of a movie/tv series affect its popularity, revenue, vote count and vote average?

Note: Throughout this project, I will be referring to tv series and movies and short films all as "movies" for the sake of simplicity.

There are about ~5600 rows with missing budget (and missing revenue as well) values given that such a high occurrence can only mean not inputing the correct data at the source of data collection. We can look manually for these values for every observation on the internet and input them ourselves but that is going to take a long time. Alternatively, we can drop these rows but that will lead to a loss of about half our dataset. Replacing them (imputing) with the mean or median would logically lead to spurious results.

In the absence of a better alternative, we simply replace the 0's with 'null' values for the budget column. We perform the same test for the revenue column and repeat this step if the no. of 0 revenue columns are as high.

Inferences¶

We could infer the following about the 4 pairs of variables chosen for this analysis:

1. Month and Revenue - If you want to maximize your revenue, the months of June and December (December moreso) are likely to be the best months for movie release.

2. Month and Popularity - If you want to maximize your popularity, the months of September and December are likely to be the best months for movie release.

3. Month and Vote count - If you want to maximize your vote count, the months of June and December are likely to be the best months for movie release.

4. Months and Vote average - If you want to maximize your vote average, the months of September and October are likely to be the best months for movie release.

Conclusions¶

Summary Report¶

This analysis of the TMDb dataset has revealed interesting insight for the questions that were posed at the beginning. These questions might be helpful for production houses and movie makers given the kind of information that we have generated from this exploration.

In summary:

Research Question 1 - Revenue-popularity and revenue-vote count seem to enjoy an inverse relationship. As counterintuitive as it may seem, a higher revenue may not lead to a higher popularity and vice-versa. Additionally, a higher revenue may not yield higher vote counts and vice-versa.

Revenue-runtime and revenue-vote average seem to enjoy a positive relationship. This implies that the higher the revenue, the higher the runtime for the movies in this data set and vice-versa. Additionally, the higher the revenue the higher the vote average which, funnily enough, seems to contradict the previous findings about revenue-vote count.

Note: The relationship between vote count and vote average have not been separately explored in this project.

Research Question 2 - The better of all months for improving a movie's revenue, popularity, and vote count is the month of December. The month of September is positively related with improving vote average. Therefore, we can say, with some probability, that December is the best month for production houses and movie makers when it comes to release. This could be attributed to a number of factors such as festival time, increase in income, appraisals, bonuses, et al.

Limitations¶

  1. The analyses may have been biased due to the no. of movies released per month particularly when understanding the best months for movie release.

  2. The dataset itself suffered from a no. of 0 values which seem to be an entry error. This may have compromised almost half the samples and resulted in poorer insights.

  3. Other factors such as cast, genres, directors, et al. have not been explored in this project. Perhaps including them would yield better, more conclusive results.

  4. At the end of the day, this is just EDA and perhaps building a model (e.g. Linear model) would give us a better picture in terms of making predictions on this data.
